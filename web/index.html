The problem

Self-custody has one fatal edge case

A wallet is controlled entirely by whoever holds the key. No bank, no reset, no court order can recover it. When the only person who knows the key is gone, the funds are frozen on-chain forever.

🔑

Keys die with you

Over 20% of all Bitcoin is estimated lost forever, much of it because the holder is gone.

👪

Families locked out

Around 60% of holders never shared access with anyone. The money is visible but unreachable.

⚖️

A will cannot help

A legal will cannot move an on-chain wallet, and probate can take many months.

How it works

Silence is the only trigger

The contract never decides that anyone died. It only measures the time since your last check-in. That simplicity is what makes it auditable.

Create your vault

Deploy your own vault, lock USDC or XLM, and name your heirs with exact shares.

Check in to stay in control

A single check-in resets the countdown. Deposit, withdraw, or change heirs any time.

Reminders keep you safe

Before every deadline you get reminders by email and phone, so a payout never triggers by accident.

Heirs are paid automatically

If you go silent past the deadline, your funds pass directly to your heirs in one atomic transaction.

Why Stellar

A new primitive, built for people

No inheritance vault like this exists on Stellar today. It is a public good that protects ordinary families, not traders.

Non-custodial by design

Keys never leave your wallet. No backend can move your funds, and the payout is permissionless, so it works even if we disappear.

Fast and near-free

Stellar settles in about five seconds with fees well under a cent, which is exactly what a long-lived, low-touch vault needs.

Exact, locked shares

Beneficiary splits are fixed in the contract, so whoever triggers the payout cannot change who gets what.

Open source and audited

The contract is small, readable, and covered by tests, with an independent security audit planned before mainnet.
